Evergreen

An uplifting data-driven campaign foused on positivity and story telling to raise awareness on climate change and forest regeneration in New Zealand.

2024

Data Driven Campaign UX/UI
The Challenge

Capstone allowed me the opportunity and freedom to create a body of work in a field within media design that explored a topic or problem I was passionate about. Coupling my expertise in UX/UI and love for the environment, this project explores how positive data-humanism-driven interactive experiences create hope to motivate young New Zealanders to protect and preserve our nature.

The Problem

Our forests and green spaces are crucial in mitigating the impacts of climate change, providing homes to 80,000 native species in New Zealand, producing oxygen and acting as carbon sinks. Furthermore, nature benefits our mental and physical well-being, providing joy and connection.

The consequences of long-overlooked climate destruction are starting to show - protecting New Zealand’s unique environment has never been more crucial than now. However, we often see information related to climate change concentrated on the doom and gloom narrative. Though highlighting the impacts of climate change is necessary, all the information can be debilitating, especially amongst Gen Z and Millennials who experience high levels of climate anxiety. A different approach is needed.

The Process

Data humanism is a human-oriented approach to collecting and presenting information. Hence, understanding my user's motives and needs was the first step. These insights helped inform my solution, providing ideas and opportunities. One core insight was that my audience had limited time for sustainable actions, proving my solution had to be convenient. A digital experience became the core part of my campaign, investigating how I could create more meaningful data visualisations on the relationship between people and nature. This participatory data collection website was developed by mapping, sketching wireframes, building prototypes, testing and refining before I began development. Consistent iteration through these steps was an integral part of the process.

However, during development, I realised the website was missing a hook. I devised many solutions to remedy this, but after recognising these features were beyond my coding capabilities, I returned to the drawing board, brainstorming how else I could communicate my message and expand my solution. Evergreen then became a campaign with new touch points that work alongside the website, supporting one another to advocate for change to protect our nature.

The Outcome

Evergreen explores how capturing personal stories and presenting them in a comprehendible, interactive and beautiful way could create hope and bring change. The website - a participatory data collection experience - allows users to reflect on what nature means to them and share this with others. It's a reflective and delightful experience that juxtaposes traditional forms of data collection by focusing on storytelling.

To expand Evergreen, social media content was designed and curated to directly reach the target audience and allow further engagement with the brand. This included a series of infographics that tell positive climate stories like how afforestation rates. Taking this positive approach provides hope, juxtaposing the idea that data related to climate change always has to be negative.

Finally, not only do forests and green spaces aid in combating climate change, but they also play a role in our well-being. An installation was created out of this insight to reconnect people with nature. Inspired by a comment left on the website, the Evergreen Pod is an urban sanctuary where users can escape the city noise in a little native forest.

Visual Design

The visual style for this project had to reflect the values of the outcome - positivity and sustainability. The brand identity is bright and connects to the environment, pulling inspiration from Aotearoa's unique nature. The bright colours paired with the playful illustrations of New Zealand native plants convey a friendly and fresh style that appeals to a younger target audience. This positive style aligns with data humanism.

In essence, Evergreen explores how a more positive and solution-focused approach to climate change can enact change. Data visualisations are the heart of the campaign. These focus more on qualitative information - personal stories and experiences that connect people. This positive approach juxtaposes the idea that data is negative and complicated. It inspires and uplifts people instead of further inducing climate anxiety. Evergreen won't solve climate change, but it begins a transition towards a more sustainable future, motivating young people to take action, no matter how small, and to plant a seed for a better future, not just for the planet's benefit, but for our own.
