Bud

2023

The Challenge

The brief required me to create an innovative design solution to a present-day, real-world problem. This project challenged me to deepen my knowledge of UX/UI Design and integrate new methodologies, allowing me to create a human-centred solution to better the world we live in.

UX/UI Graphic Web Design

The Problem

Everyone is feeling the crunch of the rising cost of living crisis with inflation hitting levels not seen since the 1980s. However, my research discovered that it is those on a lower income, often the younger generation who are completing tertiary studies or are early in their careers with lesser pay, who are struggling the most to maintain a work-life balance. Many young people lack basic financial literacy to budget, save and plan for the future. A survey and discussions with my peers confirmed that this was a main concern for my target audience. Financial education had to be the focus of my project.  

The Process

Finance has a reputation for being complicated and boring. My solution had to juxtapose this stigma to appeal to my younger audience. It also had to be accessible and convenient for this audience which is why I created a digital tool. Iterating through the design thinking process, I explored possible tools and features that would teach the basics of financial literacy engagingly. I prioritised sketching, prototyping and testing my interface to build a solution backed by research and user feedback that appeals to my audience’s needs. 

The Solution

Bud - is an easy-to-use digital platform to help people budget at a basic level. After testing my prototypes, I learned that the solution had to be straightforward. Though I could cover a plethora of information and have many features, my audience wanted simplicity. Therefore, the app kicks off the user's finance journey and teaches them the basics of financial literacy. Users can create an account to track their expenses, set saving goals and learn simple tips and tricks. Money management can be uncomplicated and empowering - this was the message I wanted to share.

The Visual Design

The coded app was accompanied by a brand package consisting of social media posts, posters, billboards and plans for a pop-up participatory installation. Creating a brand identity that was bold and playful presented finance as something inclusive, juxtaposing the mundane stigma. The vibrant colour palette reflects this positive tone of voice. Green is often associated with money and growth, contrasting it with a pop of orange creates warmth and the softer purple tones down the bright colours. The hand-drawn illustrations further the friendly vibe and represent the journey to financial freedom. The logo - Bud - also doubles as a cheerful character, your financial buddy.

Solving the challenges associated with the rising cost of living was never possible with this project. The goal is that this app will start inspiring young people to build positive money habits and to have the confidence to be their own money masters.
