Beat the Binge

A bold and daring campaign to encourage young people to slow down their drinking with interactive spaces.

2024

UX/UI Campaign Team Lead
The Challenge

It’s well known that New Zealand has a strong binge drinking culture - two out of five New Zealanders between 18 and 24 years of age drink at levels which pose a risk to themselves and others. Even though there is a lot of information and existing initiatives tackling this issue, the messages aren’t getting through to the audience. A different approach is needed.

The team - a graphic designer, lead researcher, interactive designer and myself as UX/UI designer and team leader, were challenged to create a campaign that acknowledges this harmful relationship Kiwi youth have with alcohol, exploring creative opportunities that would increase awareness of the issue and initiate a behavioural change within the target audience.

The Focus

Beat the Binge is a bold and uplifting campaign dedicated to supporting our audience in curbing binge drinking tendencies through music-themed, pop-up interactive events located around common drinking areas. Through research of our target audience’s motivations and unpacking solutions of similar subjects, we discovered a priority of our campaign should be the tone of voice.

The younger generation tends to respond poorly to accusatory and direct messaging. This is because our audience values authenticity and transparency, and aggressive tactics can be seen as intrusive. By gently steering behaviour and offering appealing, alcohol-free alternatives on a night out, we aim for a natural progression - encouraging moderation in alcohol consumption among our target demographic.

The Outcome

We crafted an energetic visual identity, brought to life with bold colours, playful illustrations and a friendly, wobbly character. Through the cheeky, relatable copyright and visual identity, an uplifting and lively voice is communicated.

As Team Leader, I was responsible for project management and organising the team, following Agile Methodologies to ensure communication. As the UX/UI Designer, I supported research that informed the identity and built the web interface following UX methodologies. This interface provided users with all the information about our campaign. The design needed to be an extension of our identity so featured animations and scroll effects to elevate the website and create an engaging movement that reflected our brand.

Our commitment lies in gently transforming the harmful alcohol consumption habits engrained in New Zealand society, especially among youth. By a form of messaging that is light and honest, we can actively engage our younger audience and start this behavioural change, shifting our attitudes and perceptions towards alcohol consumption.
